Academic Insight

While developing my report on randomness in quantum cryptography, I sought clarification from established researchers. I received helpful conceptual guidance regarding the role of randomness in Bell tests and quantum key distribution.

  • Prof. Gilles Brassard — Emphasized why pseudo-random generators are unsuitable for QKD and highlighted the importance of genuine randomness and implementation assumptions in secure quantum protocols.
  • Prof. Nicolas Gisin — Clarified the distinction between Bell-test randomness (independence assumptions) and QKD randomness (adversarial context), and pointed me toward relevant literature for deeper reading.

These exchanges were brief and purely informational, but they strengthened the theoretical foundation behind the report.
